Project overview

The use of towing kites for auxiliary propulsion of ships is increasingly attracting attention due to its potential to reduce fuel consumption and greenhouse gas emissions.

Modeling the dynamic behavior of a kite in dynamic flight is a fundamental aspect of autopilot design. This work presents in detail a 5-degree-of-freedom model based on the laws of physics and behavioral modeling of the kiteâ€™s attitude. Control strategies are crucial for ensuring efficient and safe operation. Advanced techniques such as Model Predictive Control (NMPC) and optimal control have been proposed in the literature. However, they pose computational cost challenges and are difficult to implement online. In this report, the emphasis is placed on control strategies capable of adapting to changing environmental conditions and real-time ship dynamics.

To date, all studies indicate that improving the energy efficiency of the kite involves controlling its dynamic flight within the wind window. Building upon the robust CRONE control strategy implemented by Baptiste Cadalen, we propose the combination of two feedback/feedforward control mechanisms for tracking a given trajectory in an optimal and parametrically defined manner to maximize traction along the path.
